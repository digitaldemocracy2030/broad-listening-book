# Chapter 6: The Use of Broad Listening in National Elections

A year after Takahiro Anno’s run in the Tokyo governor’s race, multiple political parties adopted broad listening in the 2025 House of Councillors election.

During the campaign period, Anno posted the following on X (formerly Twitter)[^anno-x-bl]:

> Broad listening in 2024:
> A mysterious concept promoted by an obscure fringe candidate in the Tokyo governor’s race
>
> Broad listening in 2025:
> Adopted by various political parties and effectively becoming a standard strategy

At the same time, it seemed to me that what each party was calling “broad listening” differed slightly from one case to another. That does not mean any one approach is the correct one. Rather, I believe each party, having encountered the concept of “broad listening,” was in the process of exploring better ways to communicate with people.

Each party engaged in repeated trial and error under its own distinct constraints. The insights gained from those efforts will likely prove useful, little by little, for applications in local elections and private-sector companies operating under different constraints as well. In this chapter, I focus mainly on the initiatives undertaken by various parties in the 2025 House of Councillors election.

Viewed side by side, the parties differed sharply in their collection channels, purposes, and implementation scale.

| Party | Main collection channels | Purpose / central theme | Section |
|---|---|---|---|
| Team Mirai (2025 House of Councillors election) | Talkable Manifesto (AI chat -> GitHub) | Collaborative public editing of the manifesto | 6.1 |
| Team Mirai (2026 House of Representatives election) | Voice-Reaching Manifesto (improved chat + stronger intake design) | Reducing missed proposals and strengthening the receiving side | 6.2 |
| Nippon Ishin no Kai | Kouchou AI / TTTC | Deepening the theme of social insurance premiums | 6.3 |
| Democratic Party for the People | TTTC + Kokumin AI Project + phone and mail | Using party-member and citizen voices as grounds for policy proposals | 6.4 |
| Komeito | Google Form + LLM | Developing measures to win support among younger voters | 6.5 |
| Liberal Democratic Party (started April 2026) | Jimin Voice: party-member opinion form + voices received by legislators + AI analysis[^jimin-voice] | Reflecting party members' and supporters' voices in party operations and policymaking | Not covered in this book |

Broad listening became an “effectively standard strategy” in the 2025 House of Councillors election, and by 2026 even the leading governing party, the LDP, had announced a full-scale adoption.
